How To Play

1
Press left/right arrows or A/D on your keyboard to move your slime character.
2
Press S can split your slime. This will make you Lighter. Slime can only split with states of Large and Middle.
3
Collide with other slimes to aggregate to become heavier.
4
Collide with enemies will force your slime to split. If you cannot split, you will die.
5
You can jump over enemies' heads to destroy them.
6
You can collide with the Flags in the level to move to the next level. There are three levels in the current version of the game.

Go Further

  • This is a post mortem of the game project.
  • Most of the barricades are about the physical engine. It is only when you try to implement your own engine, you will find the difficulty of simulating the real world. Unlike the Breakout game, which only includes collision between two objects, there are multiple movable objects in the Platformer game. The hero, enemies, supplies, and sometime the ammo needs to participate in the physical world. Thus there might be two or more collision happen in one update. Furthermore, there might be a collision among three objects. Here the order of collision really matters, which brings more challenge to the design of the physical engine.
  • In order to solve that, we divided the update of the physical world into multiple steps: 1. we did a pre-update of position changes according to the elapsed time regardless of the collision. 2. we looked up the collision that happened in this state, and saved the objects involved in the collision as well as the information into a vector. 3. we solved the collision by assigning reflection direction to the colliding objects and stored the information into a contact list. The contact list would be exposed to outer scope for querying the collision.
  • Separate objects in physics engine and game object in game engine. The physics engine uses fields to store a object's status of last frame and current frame. Most part of these fields are not useful for the game engine. We can create physics objects maintained by the physics engine instead of putting all those fields in game objects.
  • Apply same naming convention to all files. To make source codes and program work in all platform, we should follow a file naming convention. Use lowercase letters, number, underscore(_) and hyphen(-).
  • Luckily in this project, we prepared early to discuss the infrastructure of the project about the extending relationships and so on. Thus we could do our own job early and uninterruptedly. However, the job assignments were unbalanced as we underestimated the physical engine design, which led to a mess in the subsequent works. The physics was so unstable that it became hard to evaluate the enemy AI performance, hero movement, level reset and so on. And it was too late for the rest members to participate into the physics development. We would take this into consideration in the next project.
